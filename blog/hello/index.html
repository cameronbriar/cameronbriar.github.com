An Exciting Opportunity with Los Alamos National Laboratory

On a ship in the middle of the sea, I met a couple who work for Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL). One was a software engineer shipping code to satellites orbiting our earth, and the other was a nuclear engineer.

Without much of a physics background or knowledge of atomic energy, it was the software engineer’s role that interested me most. It sounds incredible (and incredibly frightening) to have an opportunity to deploy code that’s embedded into a floating system in space.

A few days after the ship docked back home, I received a follow-up email from the software engineer pointing to the job postings on LANL’s website. The work they’re doing is exactly the work that intrigues me. Not only is it incredibly fascinating, innovative, and on the frontier of what humans are capable of – it’s good work that’s making this place (our planet) better. 100% coinciding with the goals I’ve set forth on myself.

Contingency Plans

The work I’m currently doing is highly relevant and it’s critical that it gets documented from conception to production. If there’s any chance I’ll be able work along side these brilliant minds, I’ve got to ellicit my own and demonstrate that my B.S. (not bullshit) is in par with their PhD.
